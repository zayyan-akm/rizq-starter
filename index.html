HIES Developing markets
HSBC Islamic Global Emerging Markets Equity ETF
Exposure to high-growth developing economies. Higher potential return but also higher volatility. Not a starting point, but worth adding once you have a core position.
HIPS Europe
HSBC Islamic European Equity ETF
Shariah-screened European large caps. Adds geographic diversification away from US-heavy exposure.
HIJS Japan
HSBC Islamic Japan Equity ETF
Shariah-screened Japanese equity market. Japan is one of the world's largest economies and adds another diversification layer to a more built-out portfolio.
SKUK Islamic bonds
iShares Core Sukuk UCITS ETF
Sukuk are Islamic bonds that pay a return without interest (riba). Lower risk than equities. Adds stability to the portfolio. Good for balancing out equity exposure.
SGLN Physical gold
iShares Physical Gold ETC
Backed by physical gold held in a vault. Not technically an ETF but widely held. Gold is a store of value and an inflation hedge that holds purchasing power over time. A small allocation (5–10%) makes sense in most long-term portfolios.
